Description

Eucalyptus tereticornis trees were field-grown in large (9-m-tall) whole-tree chambers and exposed to ambient or +3 degrees C warming near Richmond, NSW, Australia. Files contain data regarding the meteorology experienced by each tree (air temperature, air humidity, incident light, etc), tree growth (diameter and stem length), tree moisture status (predawn and midday leaf water potentials), and the exchange of CO2 and H2O of leaves, branch wood, and whole tree canopies. These data files were used for the manuscript: Drake JE, MG Tjoelker, MJ Aspinwall, PB Reich, CVM Barton, BE Medlyn, R Duursma, (accepted by New Phytologist) Does physiological acclimation to climate warming stabilize the ratio of canopy respiration to photosynthesis?

Description Leaf and branch respiration measurements for a respiration partitioning project near the end of the third whole-tree chamber experiment. Sampling dates included 13May2014 (pre-girdling) and 22May2014 (post-girdling) to capture any potential girdling effect on respiration rates. Two "average" branches were detached per chamber on each date in the evening after subset by cutting at the insertion point. Branches were bagged and then processed. Branches were stripped of all leaves. The branch was cut into segments and the entire pile of branch pieces were placed into a large gas exchange chamber (Walz) for measurement of branch respiration. The leaves from that branch were mixed and three leaves were selected at random; the respiration rate of these three leaves were measured with the opaque conifer chamber (Li-6400). Everything was dried and weighed. Leaf area was measured on the three-leaf subsample. Respiration rates are expressed per unit dry mass. Variables: date: YYYY-MM-DD chamber: chamber number ("C01", C02", etc.) T_treatment: "ambient" or "elevated" branch: branch number. Branches were numbered base to apex. Description Cleaned and gapfilled chamber flux data from the third whole-tree chamber experiment (WTC3) with Eucalyptus tereticornis trees exposed to ambient or warmed (+3 deg C) conditions (n = 6). This file has been updated slightly from a previous version (a small amount of duplicate data were deleted and the start and end dates were trimmed slightly). Note that these data are hourly averages. The raw 15-minute data are also available (WTC_Temp_CM_WTCFLUX_20130910-20140530_L0_V1.csv). Six potted trees were placed in each chamber on 5 Dec 2012 and temperature treatments were initiated on 12 Dec 2012. A single tree from within each chamber was planted in the chamber center on 12 March 2013; trees were selected based on similarity in height and basal diameter across chambers within each treatment. This selection process reduced the tree-to-tree variation in initial size within treatments. Trees were irrigated regularly and equally every fifteen days with half the mean monthly rainfall (approximately). A water-exclusion treatment was added to half of the trees on 19 Jan 2014 or 12 Feb 2014, depending on your chosen definition. That is, the last day all the trees were watered was 19 Jan, while the first day that the control trees were watered but the drydown trees were not watered was 12 Feb. Trees were girdled for a separate short-term experiment on 16 May 2014 and fully destructively harvested beginning on 26 May 2014. Sensor failures and investigator entry into chambers created gaps in the flux record (~6% missing data). These missing data were manually removed (NA-filled, actually) and then gapfilled using a neural network approach as recommended by the eddy-covariance literature (Moffat et al., 2007, Ag. For. Met.). Neural networks consisted of two layers of two neurons each, informed by PAR, air temperature (TairÂ¬), tree size (square of diameter at 65-cm multiplied by height; d2h), vapour-pressure deficit (VPD), and the hour of the day. Neural networks were fit for each chamber by resilient backpropagation with weight backtracking (Riedmiller et al., 1993, IEEE) using the âneuralnetâ R package (R Development Core Team, 2012). Ten percent of the data were withheld for validation (~7000 observations); measured and gapfilled CO2 fluxes were strongly correlated with a slope that approached unity (measured flux = 0.002 + 0.913 Ã gapfilled flux, P < 0.001, r2 = 0.88), suggesting that the gapfilling procedure was robust. However the gapfilling was not as good for H2O fluxes (measured flux = 0.002 + 0.65 Ã gapfilled flux, P < 0.001, r2 = 0.64), with substantial bias (gapfilling tended to overestimate at high fluxes, whilst underestimating low fluxes). Please treat the water flux data with caution, particularly the gapfilled water fluxes. Basic Information

Name WTC_TEMP_CM_WTCFLUX-CLOSED_20140212-20140213_L1.csv
Type RAW
Size 3.6MB
File format text/plain
Description This file contains minutely data from the third whole-tree chamber experiment (WTC3) with Eucalyptus tereticornis. These data reflect closed-chamber measurements of whole-canopy respiration. Each chamber was manually transformed into a closed system and the rate of CO2 accumulation within the chamber airspace was used as a measure of whole canopy respiration. The chambers were then vented, the temperatures were changed, and measured again. In this way, whole-tree respiration was measured at 5 temperatures for each tree. These files were generated directly from the WTC flux computer and thus contain several depreciated variables that should not be used. The primary measurement of use is "CO2L". Variables: date : date in YYYY-MM-DD time : time in HH:MM:SS chamber: chamber number (1:13). Note that 13 is not actually a chamber, but reflects the measurement of outside air above the WTC demountable building. TAre: air temp for WTC original system. Not in use. Null: not in use CO2L: CO2 concentration (umol mol-1) as measured by a local irga on each chamber DiffP: Differential pressure across flow restrictor on fresh air supply (Pa) TS: not in use SoilM: not in use CO2CChamb: CO2 concentration (umol mol-1) as measured by the central Li-7000 on its last measurement. Note that this value is updated every 15-minutes, while CO2L is updated every minute. DPCChamb: dewpoint in the chamber as measured by the central Li-7000 (deg C). LCO2slope: calibration coefficient relating CO2L to CO2CChamb. We assume that CO2CChamb is the "true" value, and we update the calibration for CO2L accordingly every 15-minutes when both are measured. ValveTime: the number of seconds that pure CO2 was released into the chamber during each minute (seconds) CondWater: the amount of water condensed TBox: the instrument box temperature (deg C).
